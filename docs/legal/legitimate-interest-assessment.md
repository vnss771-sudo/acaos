# Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) — worksheet template

> For customers relying on GDPR **Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest** as their lawful basis
> for cold B2B outreach. This is a **template to help you document your own assessment** — it
> is not legal advice, and a completed LIA does not itself make any specific send lawful.
> Keep your completed LIA on file; ACAOS records only that you have attested one exists.

The three-part test (ICO-style). Complete each for your outreach programme:

## 1. Purpose test — is there a legitimate interest?
- What is the interest? (e.g. *promoting our B2B product to businesses likely to benefit.*)
  `__________________________________________________`
- Who benefits, and how? `____________________________`
- Is it lawful, specific, and real (not speculative)? `Yes / No — explain: ____________`

## 2. Necessity test — is the processing necessary?
- Is sending this outreach a reasonable way to achieve the interest? `Yes / No`
- Could you achieve it with less data / a less intrusive method? `____________________`
- How did you source the contacts, and is the volume proportionate? `________________`

## 3. Balancing test — do the individual's rights override the interest?
- Reasonable expectations: would a business contact expect this outreach given how their
  data was obtained? `____________________________________`
- Nature of the data: business contact details only? Any special-category data? `________`
- Impact on the individual; can they easily opt out? *(ACAOS includes one-click unsubscribe
  + honours suppression before every send.)* `__________`
- Safeguards applied (data minimisation, suppression, frequency caps, easy opt-out):
  `__________________________________________________`

## Outcome
- On balance, does legitimate interest apply to this programme? `Yes / No`
- Date completed: `__________` · Reviewed by: `__________` · Review again on: `__________`

> **Canada (CASL):** legitimate interest is **not** a CASL basis — you need express or implied
> consent for Canadian recipients. Record those consent bases in ACAOS (Settings → Compliance →
> consent), and flag "we send to Canadian recipients" so the send gate requires it.
